Foundation

Pixel Seasoning

Before any campaign goes live, teach the Meta pixel what good looks like. Fire all standard events and let the data accumulate before optimizing deeper down the funnel.

Core principle: The pixel optimizes toward what it has seen. The more quality signals you feed it early, the faster and cheaper every subsequent campaign will perform. Never launch cold into a conversion objective without seasoning first.
Step 1: Launch TOFU content first
What to do
  • Launch TPF (Top of Funnel) content before any conversion campaign
  • Run all funnel events across Meta and Facebook simultaneously
  • Let the pixel observe real user behavior before you ask it to optimize
  • Do not launch prospecting until pixel has meaningful data
Why it matters
  • Meta's algorithm needs signal volume to exit the learning phase
  • Optimizing for Lead before Appointment is cheaper and faster to season
  • Each downstream event becomes more accurate as upstream events accumulate
  • Prevents wasted spend during the learning phase of key campaigns
Step 2: Fire all standard pixel events

Run these events in order of ease. Start broad and work toward the final conversion. Each event seeds the algorithm with increasingly qualified signal.

Step 3: Optimization progression
Rule: Optimize for Lead until there are enough data points to go deeper. Then progressively move the optimization event further down the funnel as each stage accumulates 50+ conversions per week.
Phase 1

Optimize for Lead

Start here. Broad signal, fastest to accumulate, cheapest to collect. Run until 50+ leads/week are firing consistently.

Phase 2

Optimize for Appointment

Move here once Lead is seasoned. The algorithm now has enough data to find people likely to book, not just submit a form.

Phase 3

Optimize for Qualified Appt

Move here when Appointment volume is strong. Now targeting the highest quality signal: vetted, sales-ready prospects.

Important warning: audience separation
Do not allow overlap: When the data calls for it, prospects being served ads should not appear in both Lead Gen and Core funnels simultaneously. Irrelevant leads should not be allowed to enter both the LG and Core Subsequent funnels. Separate exclusion audiences carefully at each transition point.

Optimization Framework

Winning Ad Multiplier

Once an ad proves itself by achieving the most spend at KPI, do not just scale the budget. Multiply it. Create 4 hook variants from the winning ad and launch them in a new CBO pack to find your next winner.

Core principle: Identify your winning ad by most spend at KPI. Then systematically create shorter and longer hook variations. Each variant tests a different entry point into the same proven message, without rebuilding the ad from scratch.
The Process
Start here: your proven winner
Identified by most spend at KPI
Winning Ad #1
Now create 4 hook variants and launch in a new CBO pack
−3 sec
Trim the hook
Remove the first 3 seconds of the ad. Find your strongest hook moment and bring it to the very start. Cut any slow preamble or setup before the real hook lands.
−7 sec
Trim deeper
Remove the first 7 seconds. This is a more aggressive cut that drops further into the content where the message is already warm. Works well when the original hook is soft.
+3 sec
Add a new hook
Add a brand new 3-second hook to the front of the existing ad. The body of the ad stays identical. Only the entry point changes. Test a different pattern interrupt or opening line.
+9 sec
Add an extended intro
Add a 9-second new intro to the front of the existing ad. Use this to add more context, agitation, or curiosity before the proven message kicks in.
Launch all 4 variants together
Launch in New CBO Pack

Each variant enters the same CBO campaign. Meta allocates budget to the best performer automatically.

Why it works
Proven body, new entry

The middle and end of the ad is already validated. It converted. You are only testing different ways to hook the viewer in. Lower risk than creating new ads from scratch.

Hook is the highest leverage point

The first 3 seconds determine whether someone keeps watching. A winner with 3 different hooks can find 3× the audience who respond to different patterns of interruption.

CBO does the work

Launching all 4 variants into a new CBO pack lets Meta's algorithm find which hook version performs best. No manual budget splitting. The winning hook self-identifies.

When to trigger this
Trigger signals
  • Ad has the highest spend of any creative in the account at or below KPI
  • Ad has been running long enough to have statistical confidence (typically 7–14 days)
  • Frequency is rising. You need fresh entry points for the same audience.
  • You want to scale the message without simply raising the budget
What to do next
  • Identify the Winning Ad #1 by most spend at KPI
  • Create all 4 variants (±3s, ±7s/9s) from the same source file
  • Launch all variants in a dedicated new CBO pack
  • Let it run for 7–14 days before making decisions
  • The new winner becomes the next input to the multiplier process
