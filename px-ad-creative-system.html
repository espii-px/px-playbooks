The feedback loop
Every ad is an experiment

Each ad tests a buyer hypothesis. Win or lose, it produces data about what the market responds to.

Data becomes intelligence

CreativeOS ingests the buyer data and analyzes the patterns. It surfaces angles, language, objections, sub-niches, and winning themes.

Intelligence becomes hypotheses

The intelligence generates prioritized creative hypotheses. Each one is the next angle worth testing, briefed and ready.

Hypotheses get validated

Angle testing validates each hypothesis cheaply. Winners prove real buyer demand. Losers get killed fast.

Winners get scaled

Proven buyer psychology scales across proven formats. Iteration extends each winner further.

Results feed back in

Performance data flows back into CreativeOS as fresh buyer signals. The system gets sharper every week.

The problem this solves: The ad platform needs creative diversity to find the right buyers. Most accounts reuse the same few hooks, angles, and formats. Without a system for buyer intelligence, creative gets made reactively when performance drops. Fewer buyer signals reach the platform. Winners fatigue faster. Cost per lead and cost of acquisition rise. Lead quality declines. The sales team wastes calls. Spend gets harder to scale. The loop is how we beat it.

Phase 1 · Buyer Signals

Buyer Signals: Raw Buyer Intelligence

The raw material of the system. Everything downstream depends on the quality of the buyer data going in. These are real signals from real buyers. They are not random ideas, and not reactive ideas made only when performance drops.

Core job: Feed CreativeOS a constant stream of raw buyer intelligence. This is the language, objections, and desires of real buyers, captured from every source and ready for analysis.
Where the signals come from
First-party data
  • Meta performance data. What is winning and losing at the ad level.
  • CRM pipeline data. Which leads become buyers, and which do not.
  • Sales calls. A consistent source of top winners.
  • Team interviews. Frontline knowledge of the buyer.
  • Reviews and testimonials. Buyer language, plus proof and emotional triggers.
External sources
  • Competitor reviews. Gaps and objections in the market.
  • News and trends. Timely hooks and pattern interrupts.
  • Swipe and observation. Manual research into what works elsewhere.
Output: raw buyer signals ready for analysis. Everything here feeds Research & Intelligence →. CreativeOS scans the account, analyzes the patterns, and turns these signals into prioritized creative. Open the next tab to see it.
Human and machine CreativeOS is the engine. People should also review these sources directly. AI now surfaces genuinely useful intelligence, which was not true a few months ago. The edge is combining that speed with human judgment on what to test.
Phase 2 · CreativeOS

Research & Intelligence: CreativeOS

The intelligence engine at the center of the loop. It ingests buyer data, analyzes the patterns, and detects opportunities. It generates hypotheses, prioritizes the creative worth making, and produces the deliverables the team executes.

Core job: Turn raw buyer signals into prioritized, briefed creative. It runs automatically, every cycle. CreativeOS reads what is winning and analyzes why. It hands the team ready-to-produce briefs. A human makes the final call.
What CreativeOS does
Ingest

Pulls buyer data from every source. Performance, sales calls, reviews, competitors, and trends.

Analyze

Detects patterns in buyer psychology, language, and objections. Spots market shifts and winning themes.

Detect opportunities

Finds the angles and sub-niches with room to run before anyone else is on them.

Generate hypotheses

Turns intelligence into specific creative hypotheses. Each one is a next angle worth testing.

Prioritize

Ranks what to make next by expected impact. Effort goes where the data points.

Produce deliverables

Outputs briefs, scripts, and tagged tasks. Production-ready, every cycle.

The weekly pipeline
0 · Sync clients

Reads each client profile from a shared drive. Brand, audience, offer, and KPIs stay current without touching code.

1 · Pull performance

Pulls the last 7 days from Meta for every client. Identifies each account's top-performing ads.

Phase 3 · Validation

Angle Testing: Validating Hypotheses

The hypotheses CreativeOS generates get validated here, cheaply, before we pour effort into execution. The 10-second ad is the primary tool. It is fast to make and it converts.

Core job: Validate buyer hypotheses cheaply before pouring effort into execution. Each test proves or kills an angle by putting it in front of real buyers. We vary the sub-niche and angle, and slightly the offer, using low-effort formats first.
Primary tool
10-second ads (dedicated video placements, all platforms)
Also test angles with
Native-news statics (static placements add placement diversity)
What we vary
Sub-niche · Angle · (slightly) the Offer
Cadence
Set by budget. A small account and a large account test very differently.
Who sets volume
The performance lead sets how many new angles to test each month.
Default for new launches
10-second ads plus native-news statics on the same angles
What counts as a winning angle
The definition: The angle hits the KPI the platform optimizes for, the cost per result, and stays there across several results. Judge it by results, not days. Around 5 results at KPI is a useful benchmark, because accounts spend at very different rates.
How we call it
  • Starts under KPI right away. Strong angles usually keep working, and the best ones win immediately.
  • Confirm over roughly 5 results at KPI, not a set number of days.
  • Then check the downstream buyer metric by hand. Cheap leads that do not close are not winners.
Set it per account
  • Every account has its own definition of a winning ad.
  • It lives in the 90-day plan. Set the target acquisition cost, then work back to a target cost per lead.
  • A winner moves into scaling and triggers execution styles.
Why 10-second ads They are fast to make and they convert. They are primarily an angle-testing tool, not just an execution style. Running a 10-second video and a native-news static on the same angle also adds placement diversity. That means different inventory, different CPMs, and more chances to be seen.
Don't over-apply For accounts with existing winners, do not run only 10-second tests. Keep iterating on the proven winners too.
Phase 4 · Scale

Execution Styles: The Proven Formats

Scale proven buyer psychology across proven formats. Once an angle validates, rebuild that winning psychology in 1–5 proven execution styles. This prevents fatigue and reaches more buyers.

Core job: Scale one validated angle across multiple proven formats. Keep the winning sub-niche and buyer psychology. Swap only the execution style.
Proven formats: the core set
Every account is different, but these formats are proven across accounts. Once you find a winner, produce it in variations of these.
Video
  • AI Ambassador (tells a story)
  • Founder talking to camera
  • B-roll + voiceover
  • Street interview
  • 10-second ads
  • 5-frame influencer style
Static
  • Native-news style
  • Direct-to-offer (logo + bold headline + CTA)
  • DTO image + ICP circle
  • Carousel
  • Text-on-colour
Explainer & proof
  • Whiteboard
  • Napkin / flowchart explainer
  • Testimonial montage
Testing queue: candidates to graduate
New styles under test to join the proven set. Test on a few accounts. If a style works on 2 or 3, it becomes a standard.
Claymation Founder AI Podcast style
The rules
  • 80% proven, 20–30% new. Leverage what works, but always test.
  • Each account has its own winning styles. Not every format works everywhere.
  • Find a winner on your account? Share it so others can test it.
  • A style that works on 2 or 3 accounts becomes a new standard.
Signals vs North Star
  • Watch time reflects hook rate and hold rate. These are useful signals on the creative.
  • The North Star is cost of acquisition per buyer, not watch time.
  • A low watch-time ad that stays under target still runs. This is direct response, not organic content.
Testing workflow A dedicated owner runs the testing queue. They bring new styles to test, then graduate the winners into the core set. Anyone can add an execution-style idea to the queue.
Phase 5 · Optimize

Iterations: Continuous Optimization

Continuous optimization of every winner. As soon as an angle hits, we keep optimizing it with new variations, formats, and remixes. The goal is maximum mileage before it fatigues. Internally we call this "squeeze the juice."

⚡

Trigger: A winning angle. When anyone spots a winner, flag it and produce it in 3–4 more formats right away.

Core job: Get maximum mileage out of every winning angle before it fatigues. An ad can last a day or a year. You cannot predict which.
Format swaps: same winning angle, new form
Swap the format
  • Winning hook visual → turn it into a still.
  • Talking head → voiceover + B-roll.
  • Talking head → UGC remake (same script, new presenter).
  • Static or video → carousel.
  • Video script → text on a still.
Remix the parts
  • Chop the winning ad. Cut the hook and add a different opening. This is the winning ad multiplier. It makes one ad go further.
  • Recombine winners. Take the strongest hook, body, and close from different winning ads and combine them into one.
Squeeze forever Keep adding variations even while an ad is working, because fatigue is hard to predict. For example, take a winning ad, make a green-screen version, then animate the image into the video. This keeps the winner alive. The technique gets more sophisticated over time. For now, keep changing the elements.
Lens · Variables

Ad Elements to Test

Get granular. Every ad is built from components you can test independently. Any communication has a messenger, a message, and a receiver. Change one and it lands differently.

Why it matters: Saying "I love you" to your partner lands differently than saying it to a stranger. Same message, different context. The receiver, messenger, and message all shift the meaning. These are the levers.
1 · Sub-niche (receiver)
Who the message is for. The specific group.
2 · Stage of awareness
How much education they need.
3 · Message / Angle
The core idea we lead with.
4 · Messenger
The character delivering it.
5 · Proof type
How we make it believable.
6 · Offer
What they get, and how it connects to the angle.
7 · Hook
The opening that stops the scroll.
8 · Execution style
The proven format it's built in.

Lens · Money Pockets

Sub-Niches: The High-Value Pockets

This is a core way we differentiate. Inside a broad market, the highest-value buyers sit in smaller groups. They feel the problem in a specific, repeated, and emotionally distinct way.

Definition: A target market shares the broad problem. A sub-niche shares the specific situation where that problem becomes more urgent. Every market has them. You may not have found them yet.
How to uncover them
Look for repeated patterns
  • Where the problem happens
  • Who it happens to
  • What they want
  • What they've already tried
  • Why it matters now
Dimensions to slice by
  • Activity, meaning what they are doing when it happens
  • Life stage
  • Goal
  • Trigger moment
  • Failed solution
  • Severity · geography · size · psychographics

Why · Problem → Outcome

Why It Wins: The Problem & The Outcome

Why the system matters. Without a steady creative pipeline, performance decays in a predictable way. With one, creative stays healthy, lead quality improves, and acquisition costs fall.

The fatigue spiral: The same few hooks, angles, and formats → fewer buyer signals for the platform → winners fatigue faster → cost per lead and cost of acquisition rise → lead quality declines → the sales team wastes calls → spend will not scale.
Differentiation: avoid the sea of sameness
The market now
  • The category is crowded. Many competitors run the same "30 qualified leads in 10 days" offer.
  • The ad platform floods a lead with competitor ads the moment they engage.
  • If your ad looks like everyone else's, you become top-of-funnel for competitors.
How we stand out
  • Better angles, drawn from real understanding of the market and its sub-niches.
  • Memorable, distinct ads that do not look like everyone else's.
  • Build authority. Become the obvious choice, not another "get 30 leads" offer.
Nurture and retargeting: own the space
Familiarity is a powerful persuasion trigger. Once you have a lead, serve them steady video views so they do not forget you. Occupy the ad space so they see fewer competitor offers and build authority with your brand.
Retargeting
  • Optimized for conversion events. Target people who did not convert.
  • Low budget, few results per month, usually just below KPI.
  • Improves overall efficiency and closes more deals downstream.
Nurture
  • Best metric is video views and percent watched. Aim for 90%+ thruplays and 60–70% watching 75–80%.
  • No direct platform attribution, because you are not asking them to convert.
  • Educational, authority-building content keeps you top of mind.
